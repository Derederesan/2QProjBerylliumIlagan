Procrastination

Procrastination is a feeling that leads you to go on youtube and relax despite whatever you should be doing right now. It causes you to delay the act of doing something, even when your mind constantly nags you, telling you that you should be doing something else right now.

Oftentimes, we find ourselves saying that we would finally make a change. By tomorrow, we would finally finish that work that has been sitting on your table for several days, untouched. Yet when it is finally time to work on it, we procrastinate. Why does this happen?

Procrastination usually occurs when the task is either too challenging or too boring. The thought of completing our work when it is either of these things feels too painful for us, so we resort to abandoning it completely. There are ways to combat procrastination. Without further ado, here are quick tips on fighting procrastination.

Change the difficulty of your tasks.

If it’s too challenging:

1. Writing down everything you need to do

List them down and break them into smaller chunks. Have a video report that needs to get done? Break it down into a series of steps. The first step would be to think of what information you want to include in the video, create a script outline, make a first draft of the script, edit the script, practice reading the script, film the video, edit the video and pass the video report. If these steps feel like its too hard to do, break them down even more and schedule when you will do them.

2.Prepare the day before

Preparing the things you need for the tasks you will do tomorrow is a great help in getting started. If you need to read a lesson tomorrow, open up its document and let it sit on your computer. This way, it would be easier to access that topic the next day.

3.Taking a task one step at a time

When working on a task, refrain from thinking about all the other things you need to do. Schedule them for a specific time of the day or better yet, do that task first to get rid of that anxious feeling.

If it's too boring:

1. Increase urgency

Have a greater demand for your time than supply. Schedule several tasks that you need to accomplish in your day and stick to that schedule. You can also choose to set a shorter deadline for your tasks to further increase the urgency to do them.

2.Create consequences

This technique makes it more likely for you to finish your work in your day. If for example, you feel that you don’t want to work on your assignment right now, tell yourself that if you don’t get to watch your show on Netflix by a set time if you don’t complete your task.

3.Pomodoro technique

Get out a timer and set it for 15-20 minutes and fully work on that task. When it goes off, you can choose to either take a break or continue to work until you feel tired. Most of the time, people choose to continue working simply because they have already gotten started.

4.Find Balance

It’s important to realize that although it is good that you get more work done, you shouldn’t push yourself too hard. Learn to find the right balance of work and rest in order to get more done and avoid burnout. It’s easier to take breaks when you are tired than power through and take an even longer break.

Other tips:

Take care of your needs

Before you start working, assess whether or not you have everything you need. Did you prepare snacks for when you get hungry? Did you bring water with you? Do you have your charger ready if ever your device is low on battery? This is important so that you don’t feel the need to constantly get up when you’re supposed to be working on something.

Forgive yourself for prior procrastination

It’s normal to feel mad at yourself for wasting so much time doing nothing when you could have done so much more. Be kind to yourself, forgive yourself for procrastination, and take it as a lesson to move on and make up for it.
