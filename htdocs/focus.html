Focus

Focus is an important skill that we need to incorporate into our daily routine. It improves our way of life, helping us produce our best work in a short amount of time. It is a muscle, and like all muscles, it needs constant exercise to become stronger.

Getting into the state of focus:

To focus, one must be in the present moment. We should rid ourselves of thoughts of both the past and the present. We don't need to worry about the result, we must focus on what we are doing at the moment. The process of doing something is the most important aspect of focus.

Focus should not feel forced, it is a state of both calm and alertness. If we focus on getting the perfect results in our work, we will not achieve focus. We would be too busy stressing over a small detail in our work, that no one would notice. Trust the process to gain a good sense of focus.

How to focus while working:

1. Remove distractions while working

Optimize your working space to be free of distractions. Turn off your notifications, close all your unnecessary tabs, and hide your phones somewhere far away from you. Determine what distracts you and find a way to prevent this distraction from interrupting your workflow.

2. Avoid multitasking.

Multitasking is essentially switching between tasks. It tires your brain faster and makes you less likely to finish your work. It also gives you attention residue, a tendency of your mind to think about what you were doing after switching tasks. For example, you reach for your phone while writing a research essay. You scroll a little on Instagram and look at puppies, then return to your work. While you’re busy writing your essay, your mind can’t help but wander and think about the puppies you saw on Instagram. This is attention residue.

3. Take small breaks

Resist the temptation to multitask and learn to take real breaks when you feel tired. Step away from your computer and take a short break. You can stretch, walk around and eat a little. Avoid using your phone when you aren’t done with your tasks yet to prevent mindlessly scrolling and forgetting about your task.

Training focus:

We can train our focus by practicing on things that require our attention. These activities could range from listening to lectures, paying attention to youtube videos, etc. Make sure that you avoid going through the comments while watching a youtube video. We should also pay attention to what we consume. If we normally watch videos that are short and stimulating, we must make sure to balance them with content that is long with little stimulation. These could be content such as ted talks, podcasts, blog posts, etc.
